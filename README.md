**# Doctoral Meta-Analyses 2: Ethnomedicine & Ethnopharmacology**

A curated collection of comprehensive, doctoral-level meta-analyses exploring traditional plant medicines, their pharmacological potential, and modern scientific validation.

### Overview

This repository compiles in-depth, synthesized literature reviews on ethnomedicine and ethnopharmacology. Each document functions as a standalone scholarly meta-analysis suitable for academic reference, research inspiration, thesis development, or interdisciplinary study in pharmacognosy, natural products chemistry, and integrative medicine.

**Series Focus**: Bridging indigenous knowledge systems with contemporary evidence-based research on medicinal plants, their bioactive compounds, bioavailability, mechanisms, and discovery strategies.

### Contents

- **Phytochemical Bioavailability**  
  Evaluating molecular extraction efficiency and absorption rates of major traditional plant alkaloid groups (isoquinoline, purine, pyridine, quinoline, tropane, etc.).

- **Comparative Ethnopharmacology**  
  Cross-referencing disparate indigenous medicine systems (Native American, African, Ayurvedic, TCM, European folk, Amazonian) to identify overlapping therapeutic plant selections and ethnobotanical convergence.

- **Neuro-Modulatory Botanicals**  
  Aggregating clinical and preclinical evidence on natural adaptogens (*Withania somnifera*, *Rhodiola rosea*, *Panax ginseng*, *Schisandra*, etc.) and their effects on the HPA axis, stress resilience, cognition, and CNS function.

- **Antimicrobial Natural Products**  
  Meta-analysis of minimum inhibitory concentrations (MIC) of plant-derived terpenes and terpenoids (thymol, carvacrol, eugenol, etc.) against bacterial and fungal pathogens.

- **Bioprospecting Predictive Modeling**  
  Using phylogenetic, ethnobotanical, and geospatial metadata to build statistical maps for prioritizing high-viability geographic biomes and taxa in natural product discovery.

### Purpose

- Provide high-quality, synthesized overviews that connect traditional knowledge with modern pharmacokinetics, phytochemistry, and data-driven discovery methods.
- Serve as a starting point for researchers, students, and practitioners in ethnopharmacology, herbal medicine, drug discovery, and conservation.
- Highlight patterns of convergence across cultures and the scientific rationale behind traditional uses.

### Keywords

ethnopharmacology, ethnomedicine, medicinal plants, adaptogens, alkaloids, terpenes, bioprospecting, phytochemical bioavailability, traditional medicine, meta-analysis, natural products, ethnobotany, predictive modeling

### Methodology Note

Each analysis employs narrative synthesis of peer-reviewed literature, systematic review principles, and where applicable, aggregated quantitative data (e.g., MIC ranges, cortisol reductions, model performance metrics). Documents are written in formal academic style with sections typical of doctoral-level work (Abstract, Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusions).

### Disclaimer

These documents were generated as comprehensive literature syntheses for educational and research discussion purposes. They are not substitutes for original peer-reviewed publications or professional medical advice. Readers should consult primary sources and conduct their own due diligence.
